# Hidden Champion Definition (KHC-DE)

## Purpose
This document defines strict inclusion and exclusion criteria so the Discovery Engine can consistently output candidates that represent **operational excellence + invisibility to the startup ecosystem**.

---

## Inclusion criteria (Required)
A company is a **Hidden Champion** only if it satisfies all required conditions.

1. **Kenya-founded or Kenya-based operations**
   - Incorporated in Kenya, or
   - Operates materially in Kenya (customers, staff, warehouses/branches).

2. **Operating for 5+ years**
   - Exceptions allowed if there’s strong evidence of continuity (e.g., successor of a legacy business).

3. **Strong customer trust signals**
   Evidence may include:
   - Google Maps reviews (quantity + rating)
   - Testimonials/case studies
   - Repeated institutional references (NGOs, corporates, government)
   - Consistent word-of-mouth signals (reviews, directories, news)

4. **No major startup-ecosystem visibility**
   - Not primarily known as a venture-backed startup.
   - Not a frequent media/VC/accelerator darling.

---

## Exclusion criteria
If any exclusion condition is met, the company is **rejected** or **sent to secondary review**.

1. **Raised venture capital**
   - Any VC round, investor announcements, “seed/series A” type visibility.

2. **Frequently featured by mainstream startup/media outlets**
   - TechCabal, Disrupt Africa, TechCrunch, etc.
   - Regular “startup spotlight” coverage.

3. **Accelerator alumni**
   - Explicit alumni of accelerators/incubators that indicate startup ecosystem positioning.

4. **Startup competition regulars**
   - Frequent pitching/competition wins/participation.

5. **Heavy LinkedIn presence**
   - Not merely an official page, but consistent “startup branding” activity.
   - (Measured via evidence; never rely on vibes.)

6. **Category exclusions**
   - Explicit: “AI startup”, “fintech startup”, “Web3 startup”
   - If the business is actually a service provider/supplier (not a startup play), it can be eligible.

---

## Evidence rules (How to decide)
For every inclusion/exclusion claim, capture at least one evidence artifact:
- URL (review page, news, directory listing)
- screenshots/notes from Google Maps
- procurement page
- association membership page
- website “about” or “history” page

---

## Scoring alignment
The Ecosystem Visibility detector should produce a visibility score.
- **High visibility → more likely exclusion**
- Evidence-based gating overrides scoring if exclusion criteria are strongly confirmed.

---

## Output classification states
- **Qualified Hidden Champion**
- **Near Miss (rejected by one rule)**
- **Needs Validation (insufficient evidence)**
- **Rejected (exclusion evidence confirmed)**
