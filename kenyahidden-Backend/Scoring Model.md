# Scoring Model — KHC-DE (Out of 100)

## Overview
Hidden Champion Score is computed as:

- **Longevity (20)**
- **Customer Trust (20)**
- **Operational Footprint (20)**
- **Growth Signals (20)**
- **Ecosystem Invisibility (20)**

Total = **100**

---

## 1) Longevity (20)
Goal: reward operational continuity.

- 0–5 years → 5
- 5–10 years → 10
- 10–20 years → 15
- 20+ years → 20

Exception handling:
- If “successor” evidence exists, allow a capped adjustment with notes.

---

## 2) Customer Trust (20)
Evidence sources:
- Google reviews (rating + count)
- testimonials/case studies
- repeat customers (if publicly visible)

Suggested rubric:
- Minimal evidence → 5–8
- Strong evidence → 12–16
- Very strong trust signals → 18–20

(Implementation note: document thresholds you use.)

---

## 3) Operational Footprint (20)
Signals:
- branches
- employees estimate
- fleet/warehouse/distribution

Suggested approach:
- 0–1 branch → low
- 2–3 branches → mid
- 4+ branches / multi-site distribution → high

---

## 4) Growth Signals (20)
Signals:
- hiring activity (if visible)
- new locations
- expanded product lines
- exports

Suggested approach:
- slow/no evidence → 6–10
- moderate evidence → 11–15
- strong evidence → 16–20

---

## 5) Ecosystem Invisibility (20)
Goal: subtract “startup-ecosystem presence”.

- 0 startup visibility → 20
- heavy startup visibility → 0

Evidence flags:
- VC funding announcements
- accelerator alumni
- frequent startup media features
- heavy LinkedIn startup branding
- AI/fintech/Web3 categorization

---

## Classification thresholds
- **90–100:** Exceptional
- **80–89:** Hidden Champion
- **70–79:** Emerging Champion
- **60–69:** Watchlist
- **<60:** Reject

---

## Evidence requirements (must-have)
For any candidate scoring ≥80:
- longevity evidence (years operating)
- customer trust evidence
- ecosystem visibility evidence OR explicit “insufficient evidence—needs validation”
